Star Trek's solitons are real

Geordi La Forge was right: solitons are exciting—no matter what his colleagues may think. A soliton is a pulse that experiences both nonlinearity and dispersion. Nonlinearity sharpens the pulse—taller parts travel faster than shorter parts (like a wave breaking on a beach). Dispersion spreads it out—different frequencies travel at different speeds. With a soliton the sharpening and spreading balance exactly (and the balance is stable!).
